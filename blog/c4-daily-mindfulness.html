Daily Mindfulness

What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?
I learned that the way we interpret what others say may differ greatly from what they actually mean. It took a few ‘loops’ to figure things out but after that I felt like we both felt understood. I found it pretty hard to talk for 3-4 minutes so inevitably there was a bit of silent mindfulness going on. ‘Dipping’ wasn’t too difficult though because I feel like I do this already, when I’m listening to someone I take note of how I’m feeling pretty regularly. The other person who is a friend, thought it was an interesting idea and was open to trying it. At times I think it was a bit hard for both of us to articulate exactly what the other person meant, but they seemed to enjoy it.

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?
I like the idea of walking meditation because I think I take simple things like walking and my health for granted sometimes. I can practice informal mindful conversation on a daily basis by giving the other person my undivided attention when listening. Mingyur Rinpoche says we can do a bit less than what we are capable of to make meditation feel like less of a chore. Meng also says that one mindful breath a day is also a viable form of practicing, and I think I’m more than capable of doing that!
